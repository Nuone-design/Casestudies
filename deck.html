Reflection, learnings & what's next

This project changed how I work. Not just what I shipped

Claude Code didn't always get it right
Some components came out visually broken or craft-wise not good enough. I had to rebuild skills, create new ones, experiment with Figma integrations. Each failure made the system tighter
Be concrete, not clever
The more precise and well-structured my inputs, skills, principles, explorations. Efficiency with Claude Code comes from rigour upfront, not from prompting harder
Resourcefulness
This project made me genuinely AI-native. Not as a label — as a daily workflow. I shifted from Figma as my primary tool to Claude Code almost instantly

I got kudos from our SVP of Engineering and the wider engineering team, not for the design but for how it was built

What's next
  • 01Complete the remaining 30%: full environment QA, fix any broken states, roll out to full demo shop

  • 03Internal prototype playground: already asked to build the next enabler so teams can explore and test capabilities without needing a designer in the loop

Discovery

Discovery and validation

  • 01Synthesised all internal and external research and market data
  • 02Profiled against ICP data to find genuine product-market matches
  • 03Structured customer discovery calls across multiple verticals
  • 04Mapped seller and buyer journeys separately. They were not the same problem
  • 05Daily synthesis shared with CPO. Full transparency throughout

Buyers were consistent. Sellers were chaos. Every customer ran different systems, cultures, processes.

The assumptions were wrong

Interest is not readiness. Enthusiasm is not adoption.

The recommendation

The recommendation

  • 01Our ICP is not ready. Building ahead of readiness compounds the cost
  • 02Framed as business risk anchored to comparable project scope north of €2M
  • 03Collaborated with the team to model integration, resource and roadmap costs
